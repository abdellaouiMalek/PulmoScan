Understanding Lung Cancer Stages

Lung cancer staging is crucial for determining the appropriate treatment and prognosis. Our AI model can help identify the stage based on CT scan analysis.

Stage I

Cancer is localized to the lung without lymph node involvement.

Stage II

Cancer has spread to nearby lymph nodes or invaded chest structures.

Stage III

Cancer has spread to lymph nodes or structures further from the original tumor.

Stage IV

Cancer has spread to other parts of the body (metastasized).

About Our Model

3D ResNet-18 for Cancer Staging

Our model is a 3D adaptation of the ResNet-18 architecture, specifically trained on lung CT scans to classify cancer stages. The model analyzes the entire 3D volume to make accurate predictions.

Key Features:
  • Trained on the NSCLC-Radiomics dataset with over 400 annotated CT scans
  • Achieves 87% accuracy in distinguishing between cancer stages
  • Processes full 3D volumes to capture spatial relationships
  • Provides confidence scores for each prediction
  • Optimized for clinical use with fast inference time
